Adjust Letter Borders

The left and right lines sit on each letter's edges — where the next letter butts up against it. Drag a line outward to give a cramped letter (like “i”) more room, inward to tighten a wide one (like “T”). 0 = the line is right on the edge.

Measures each letter's shape and sets the borders so every letter feels the same width — narrow letters (i, l) get more breathing room, letters with long arms (T, L) get tucked in. Your saved widths aren't touched until you hit “Apply & rebuild”.
